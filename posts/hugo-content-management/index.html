名词

Page resources – images, other pages, documents, etc. – have page-relative URLs and their own metadata.

Page Bundles are a way to group Page Resources.

Page Bundle

definition

A Page Bundle can be one of:

  • Leaf Bundle(leaf means it has no children)
  • Branch Bundle(home page, section, taxonomy terms, taxonomy list)

examples

content/
├── about(Leaf Bundle)
│   ├── index.md
├── posts
│   ├── my-post(Leaf Bundle)
│   │   ├── content1.md
│   │   ├── content2.md
│   │   ├── image1.jpg
│   │   ├── image2.png
│   │   └── index.md
│   └── my-other-post(Leaf Bundle)
│       └── index.md
│
└── another-section
    ├── ..
    └── not-a-leaf-bundle
        ├── ..
        └── another-leaf-bundle(Leaf Bundle)
            └── index.md

content/
├── branch-bundle-1(Branch Bundle)
│   ├── branch-content1.md
│   ├── branch-content2.md
│   ├── image1.jpg
│   ├── image2.png
│   └── _index.md
└── branch-bundle-2(Branch Bundle)
    ├── _index.md
    └── a-leaf-bundle
        └── index.md

section

A Section is a collection of pages that gets defined based on the organization structure under the content/ directory.

archetypes(原型)

taxonomy(分类系统)

Hugo includes support for user-defined groupings of content called taxonomies. Taxonomies are classifications of logical relationships between content.

front matter(metadata)

  • TOML
    identified by opening and closing +++.
  • YAML
    identified by opening and closing —.
  • JSON
    a single JSON object surrounded by ‘{’ and ‘}’, followed by a new line.
